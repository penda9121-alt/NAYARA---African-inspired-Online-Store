# NAYARA---African-inspired-Online-Store

## Inspiration

Nayara was inspired by my passion for African culture, fashion, beauty, and creativity. I wanted to create an online store that sells traditional and modern clothing, bags, shoes, jewelry, and beauty products from West Africa, specifically Burkina Faso, Mali, and Senegal.

The goal of this project is to showcase and promote the culture, craftsmanship, and handmade masterpieces from these countries. The products featured in the store include handmade bags, dresses, traditional and contemporary shoes, bayas (waist beads), woussoulan (incense), jewelry, and beauty products made in Burkina Faso using local ingredients.

Each item reflects authentic African artistry, vibrant fabrics, unique designs, and cultural identity that are often underrepresented in mainstream stores.

## The Problem

I chose this type of store because I frequently hear negative stereotypes about my countries that do not reflect reality. Through this project, I wanted to highlight the richness, creativity, elegance, and talent that exist within these cultures.

Many people believe that fashionable or high-quality products must come from abroad, while in reality West African artisans create beautiful handmade products that deserve international recognition.

I also wanted to create a platform that celebrates African culture in a modern and positive way while giving visibility to local craftsmanship and creativity.


## How I Came Up With the Idea

As someone originally from Burkina Faso, this project is very personal to me. I wanted to combine my interest in technology, design, and fashion with my cultural background.

While brainstorming ideas for my web programming project, I realized I wanted to build something meaningful that represented my identity and values. I imagined an elegant online store where people could discover African-inspired products while also learning to appreciate the beauty and uniqueness of West African culture.

I also wanted to inspire younger generations, including my siblings, to feel proud of their heritage and traditions.


## Why These Products

I chose these products because they are meaningful, artistic, and unique. Many of them are handmade or custom-made, which makes every piece special and personal.

These products perfectly embody African culture, traditions, and identity while also being modern, fashionable, and appealing to a wider audience.

In addition, supporting these products means supporting local artisans and helping preserve traditional skills that have been passed down for generations.


## Challenges

One of the biggest challenges during the development of Nayara was organizing and managing multiple pages, products, and files while keeping the website visually consistent and easy to navigate.

Another challenge was implementing the shopping cart functionality using JavaScript and LocalStorage. Making sure products were correctly added to the cart, stored properly, and displayed dynamically required debugging and problem-solving.

I also faced challenges with:
- Organizing product data and images efficiently
- Managing file structures and navigation between pages
- Creating responsive and visually balanced layouts
- Keeping the website aesthetic consistent across all sections
- Debugging JavaScript functionality and cart behavior

Through these challenges, I improved both my technical and problem-solving skills while learning how important organization and testing are in web development.
